Lean Strategy Within CAD$400 Budget

Given the budget constraint, a lean strategy was developed to maximize leads for Rekha, focusing on cost-effective tactics:

1. Local SEO and Website Optimization

Update HarmonyPlan.ca with keywords like "life insurance Hamilton," "best life insurance Hamilton," etc. (free, DIY). Create one blog post (300–500 words) titled "Why Hamilton Families Need Life Insurance" to attract organic traffic, costing $50–$100 if outsourced. Expected impact: Local SEO drives 46% of Google searches, per Moz's Local SEO Guide, generating 5–10 organic leads/month in Hamilton's market.

2. Targeted Meta Ads with Retargeting

Allocate $250 for Meta Ads (Facebook/Instagram), targeting Hamilton residents (ages 25–65, interests in family planning, homeownership). Use a single ad set with a video or carousel showcasing Rekha's story: "I'm Rekha, your Hamilton insurance agent, here to protect your family's future." Offer a lead magnet: "Download Our Free Hamilton Life Insurance Guide" linked to a simple HarmonyPlan.ca form.

Install the Facebook Pixel to retarget visitors who don't convert, creating one retargeting ad: "Still need life insurance? Get a free quote from Rekha in Hamilton!" with a 14-day audience window.

Budget: $250/month ($8–$10/day), aiming for a CPL of $10–$15, expecting 15–25 leads/month, with 1–2 conversions at $1,000–$2,000 premium value.

Impact: Meta Ads are cost-effective for Hamilton's smaller market, and retargeting lowers CPL by re-engaging site visitors, per Upbeat Agency's Retargeting Insights.

3. Community Engagement

Join one local Facebook group (e.g., "Hamilton Community Hub") and share a post introducing Rekha: "Hi Hamilton! I'm Rekha, your local FSRA-licensed insurance agent. DM me for a free insurance tip!" (free, ensure compliance with group rules and CASL anti-spam laws).

Distribute 100 flyers at a local event (e.g., Hamilton Public Library or Mulberry Coffeehouse) with a QR code linking to HarmonyPlan.ca's quote form, designed using Canva (free) or costing $50 for a designer, plus $50 for printing.

Cost: $50–$100.

Impact: Community engagement builds trust, potentially driving 5–10 referral leads/month, as 88% of consumers trust local recommendations, per BrightLocal's Local Consumer Review Survey.

4. Email Follow-Up

Use Mailchimp (free tier, supports up to 500 contacts) to send a three-email sequence to leads from the Meta Ads form: (1) Welcome with guide download, (2) Testimonial from a Hamilton client, (3) Offer a free 15-minute consultation.

Cost: $0.

Impact: Email nurturing converts 10–15% of leads, with open rates of 20–30%, per Mailchimp's Email Marketing Benchmarks.
